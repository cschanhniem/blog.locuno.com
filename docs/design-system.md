## 1. Core Thesis

`Locuno NextGen` must stop reading like a polished edtech marketing surface and start reading like a premium, high-trust, future-facing assessment and formation system.

The visual direction is:

- not startup SaaS
- not generic AI luxury
- not soft wellness education
- not neon futuristic concept art

The visual direction must become:

- `premium assessment dossier`
- `editorial luxury with restraint`
- `confidential family intelligence service`
- `quiet authority`
- `human-led technology`

North star:

> Academy should feel like a private, beautifully made dossier for a child's future, where every screen looks authored, measured, and expensive in the way rare institutions feel expensive: through restraint, materiality, and confidence.

## 2. Relationship To The Main Locuno System

The broader marketing system still takes `/one` as the chromatic anchor. Academy does not reject that. Academy refines it.

Rules:

- `Locuno blue` stays in the system, but only as a controlled signal color.
- Academy does not use blue as ambient spectacle.
- The main mood comes from charcoal, warm paper, and restrained copper.
- Blue should appear in CTA emphasis, focus states, progress markers, and selected highlights, not as glowing atmosphere.

In short:

- `/one` gives Academy structural discipline, density, and CTA posture.
- The envelope reference gives Academy its material language, emotional tone, and image world.

## 3. Brand Feeling

Academy must evoke:

- trust
- seriousness
- privacy
- ritual
- calm precision
- authored taste
- intellectual depth
- parental confidence

Academy must never evoke:

- hype
- acceleration theater
- gadget worship
- pseudo-luxury
- glossy AI futurism
- “innovation wallpaper”

## 4. Visual Archetype

The approved Academy archetype is:

- `Editorial Luxury` as the primary vibe

Interpreted through:

- charcoal paper
- warm inserts
- museum-grade spacing
- severe typography
- dossier-like stacking
- shadow as weight, not as decoration

Approved layout archetypes for Academy:

- `Editorial Split` for heroes and closing bands
- `Asymmetrical Dossier Grid` for image-heavy sections
- `Linear Evidence Rail` for logic, steps, outcomes, and parent-facing proof

Rejected layout archetypes:

- symmetrical 3-column SaaS grid as default
- edge-to-edge product dashboard layouts
- card soup
- centered everything

## 5.2. Color rules

- Dark surfaces should dominate the emotional center.
- Light surfaces should feel like paper, not like generic website white.
- Copper is an accent, not a fill color.
- Signal blue is functional, not atmospheric.
- Purple must not appear.
- Cyan glow must not appear.

## 5.3. Background recipes

- Main dark field: `linear-gradient(180deg, #151515 0%, #101010 100%)`
- Warm paper field: `linear-gradient(180deg, #F4EFE7 0%, #EEE4D7 100%)`
- Neutral quiet field: `linear-gradient(180deg, #FAF8F4 0%, #F3EEE6 100%)`
- Charcoal panel field: `linear-gradient(180deg, #1D1D1D 0%, #141414 100%)`

Allowed texture overlays:

- film grain
- paper tooth
- subtle soft-touch board noise

Forbidden textures:

- shiny chrome reflections
- glassmorphism fog over full sections
- synthetic mesh gradients

## 6. Typography

This is an Academy-specific recommendation. It describes the intended direction, whether or not every font is implemented immediately.

## 6.1. Typeface roles

- Display serif: `Canela`, `Ivar Display`, or `PP Editorial New`
- Display grotesk: `Clash Display` or `Neue Haas Grotesk Display`
- Body/UI: `Geist`, `Plus Jakarta Sans`, or `Suisse Int'l`
- Mono/meta: `IBM Plex Mono` or `JetBrains Mono`

If only one premium pair is chosen:

- Headings: `Clash Display`
- Body/UI: `Geist`

## 6.2. Typography character

Headlines:

- tight
- large
- high-contrast
- minimal line count
- more like a cover line than a SaaS headline

Body:

- quiet
- disciplined
- never overly airy
- never chatty

Meta labels:

- uppercase
- micro-size
- wide tracking
- mono or highly structured sans

## 6.3. Typography rules

- Do not use Inter, Roboto, Arial, Open Sans, or Helvetica for Academy-facing hero and premium landing surfaces.
- Avoid generic italicized “luxury” styling everywhere. Use emphasis selectively.
- Never pair an ornate serif with a noisy image. If typography rises, imagery must simplify.
- Body text must read as calm editorial prose, not as product-copy UI filler.

## 6.4. Recommended scale

| Role | Desktop | Mobile |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hero H1 | `72-96px` | `42-54px` |
| Section H2 | `48-64px` | `30-38px` |
| Card title | `24-32px` | `20-24px` |
| Lead paragraph | `18-20px / 1.7` | `16-18px / 1.75` |
| Body paragraph | `15-17px / 1.75` | `14-16px / 1.75` |
| Eyebrow | `10-12px` | `10-11px` |

## 7. Spacing And Rhythm

Academy must breathe harder than the default marketing site.

### 7.1. Section rhythm

- Desktop section padding: `py-28` to `py-40`
- Mobile section padding: `py-16` to `py-20`
- Hero top padding must feel ceremonial, not merely practical
- Do not stack sections too tightly

### 7.2. Internal rhythm

- Headline to body: `20-28px`
- Body to actions: `28-40px`
- Card padding: `24-40px`
- Image to caption/meta: `16-20px`

### 7.3. Whitespace philosophy

- If a block feels “efficient,” it is probably not premium enough.
- Academy should never look cramped.
- Density should come from composition and hierarchy, not from over-packing.

## 8. Surfaces And Shells

Academy must use nested, machined, dossier-like shells.

## 8.1. Double-bezel rule

Major cards and image frames should use two layers:

- Outer shell:
  - subtle ring or border
  - supporting fill
  - generous radius
  - low-contrast separation
- Inner core:
  - true content surface
  - slightly smaller radius
  - own highlight or inner shadow

The goal is not skeuomorphism.
The goal is to make every important container feel fabricated, deliberate, and premium.

## 8.2. Radius system

- Macro shell radius: `28-40px`
- Inner shell radius: `calc(outer - 6px to 10px)`
- Buttons: fully rounded pills
- Minor chips: `999px`

Avoid:

- small `12px` app-card corners
- generic dashboard geometry

## 8.3. Border system

Use:

- paper-edge lines
- white/10 on dark
- black/6 to black/10 on light

Avoid:

- default gray 1px borders
- heavy divider grids everywhere

## 8.4. Shadow system

Use:

- ambient, diffused, expensive
- shadow as depth cue

Avoid:

- harsh e-commerce shadows
- obvious card drop shadows

Recommended tone:

- light surfaces: `0 20px 60px rgba(20, 20, 20, 0.06)`
- dark surfaces: `0 20px 60px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.24)`

## 9. CTA System

Academy CTAs should feel like premium instruments, not colored web buttons.

## 9.1. Primary CTA

- full pill
- larger horizontal padding
- signal blue as action anchor
- nested trailing icon in a circular island
- slight internal tension between text block and icon island

## 9.2. Secondary CTA

- paper or outline treatment
- same silhouette as primary
- never look like the fallback “ghost button” from a component library

## 9.3. Dark-surface CTA

- can be white-on-charcoal or paper-on-dark
- still needs nested icon island
- never flatten into a low-contrast translucent button

## 9.4. CTA language

CTA text must sound:

- clear
- calm
- irreversible in value, not in pressure

Avoid:

- urgency hacks
- generic “Learn more”
- startup verbs

## 10. Motion System

Academy motion must feel weighted, premium, and nearly physical.

## 10.1. Motion character

- slow enough to feel costly
- fast enough to remain modern
- never bouncy
- never playful

## 10.2. Approved easing

- `cubic-bezier(0.32, 0.72, 0, 1)`
- `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)`

## 10.3. Approved animation types

- fade-up with gentle y shift
- masked reveal
- object scale from `0.985` to `1`
- icon-island diagonal drift
- image hover with slight crop tension

## 10.4. Forbidden animation types

- linear transitions
- instant state changes
- bounce
- elastic overshoot
- large parallax
- scroll event-driven transforms
- floating glow pulsation

## 10.5. Motion timing

- micro interaction: `500-700ms`
- section reveal: `800-1000ms`
- hover crop shift: `900-1200ms`

## 11. Image Language

Academy imagery must not be treated as decoration.

The image system is already defined in:

- [academy.md](/Volumes/SSD/locuno/www.locuno.com/docs/arts/academy.md)
- [assessment.md](/Volumes/SSD/locuno/www.locuno.com/docs/arts/assessment.md)

This document defines how imagery must behave inside the UI.

## 11.1. Approved image language

- editorial still-life photography
- tactile materials
- dossier objects
- marked-up paper
- notebooks, folders, inserts, seals
- controlled human traces
- muted screens used as tools, never as spectacle

## 11.2. Rejected image language

- portals
- crystals
- cyberpunk glow
- glossy CGI metaphors
- abstract busts and masks
- floating holograms
- generic “AI future” symbolism

## 11.3. Image placement rules

- every large image must earn its place
- a page can be premium with very few images
- if text already carries the weight, image should anchor, not duplicate

## 12. Layout Patterns

## 12.1. Hero pattern

Preferred:

- editorial split
- massive left-side headline
- right-side image or dossier artifact
- evidence rail or supporting statements beneath

Not preferred:

- centered SaaS hero
- floating screenshot alone

## 12.2. Evidence rail

Use for:

- assessment dimensions
- process steps
- outcomes
- parent-facing proof

Pattern:

- vertical rhythm
- left border or sequence marker
- numbered editorial cadence

## 12.3. Dossier grid

Use for:

- image mosaics
- program bands
- multi-part assessment visuals

Pattern:

- asymmetry
- contrasting block scales
- enough whitespace to keep hierarchy intact

## 12.4. Dark authority band

Use for:

- page pivots
- moral thesis
- process seriousness
- final CTA

Must include:

- deep charcoal field
- carefully managed contrast
- no cheap transparency haze

## 13. Component Behavior

## 13.1. Header

Academy keeps the shared `LandingHeader`, but the surrounding page must make that shared shell feel intentional.

Rules:

- header can remain light and detached
- first section beneath it must create the tonal shift into Academy world
- do not make Academy feel like a separate microsite
- do make Academy feel like the most premium lane inside the Locuno system

## 13.2. Cards

Cards should not all look alike.

Approved card families:

- dossier cards
- editorial note cards
- dark thesis cards
- evidence cards

Avoid repeating a single generic card treatment across the entire page.

## 13.3. Icons

Icons are secondary in Academy.

Use:

- ultra-light line icons
- structured mono labels
- number-led systems instead of icon-led systems when possible

Avoid:

- Lucide-heavy grids as the primary visual rhythm
- thick default iconography

## 13.4. Dividers

Use dividers as editorial pacing tools, not as leftover UI chrome.

Good:

- top rules
- left rails
- subtle section breaks

Bad:

- boxes around everything
- unnecessary row separators

## 14. Page-Specific Guidance

## 14.1. `/academy`

This is the flagship Academy entry surface.

It should feel:

- monumental
- editorial
- confident

It must include:

- one striking entry artifact
- disciplined program logic
- visible assessment-first wedge

It must not feel:

- like a stack of generic marketing sections

## 14.2. `/academy/assessment`

This page is the most confidential and serious.

It should feel:

- darker
- more precise
- more private
- more trust-heavy than all other Academy pages

It should use:

- fewer images
- stronger hero artifact
- more evidence rails

Reference:

- [assessment.md](/Volumes/SSD/locuno/www.locuno.com/docs/arts/assessment.md)

## 14.3. `/academy/about`

This page carries philosophy.

It should feel:

- larger in thought
- still tactile
- less dark than assessment
- more reflective than program pages

It must not drift into abstract spiritual futurism with no material anchor.

## 14.4. `/academy/how-it-works`

This page should read like an operating model from a premium institution.

It should feel:

- intelligent
- systematic
- image-led only where it clarifies method

It must not read like a feature page for an AI tool.

## 14.5. `/academy/programs/*`

Program pages should feel like:

- different dossiers from one family
- each with its own emphasis
- all governed by the same material system

Program differentiation:

- `Assessment`: confidential, diagnostic, precise
- `Reset`: focused, restorative, depth-over-speed
- `Flagship`: rigorous, crafted, high-commitment

## 14.6. `/academy/mentors`

Mentors page is about standards, not celebrity glamour.

Rules:

- do not AI-generate mentor portraits
- do not over-style reference photography
- let shell and pacing do the premium work

## 15. Anti-Slop Absolute Zero

If Academy contains any of the following, the design fails:

- Inter/Roboto/Arial-centric premium surfaces
- Lucide-heavy visual identity
- generic gray borders
- default shadows
- neon glow atmospherics
- glassmorphism over content sections
- portal or crystal imagery
- stock-photo family smiling into camera
- dashboard cards masquerading as editorial layout
- random premium clichés with no relation to assessment, mentorship, or evidence

## 16. Implementation Doctrine

Before shipping Academy-facing design changes, check:

- Does this look like part of the same family as the envelope reference?
- Does this preserve Locuno’s blue as signal rather than spectacle?
- Does this feel more like a private dossier than a startup landing page?
- Is the composition expensive because of restraint, not because of decoration?
- Are we adding fewer but stronger elements?

## 17. Done Criteria

Academy design is correct when:

- it feels premium before it feels expressive
- it feels private before it feels loud
- it feels human-led before it feels technological
- it feels authored before it feels generated
- it feels like one coherent campaign across root, assessment, about, and programs

Final test:

> If a parent opened this page and felt “someone serious designed a system for my child’s future,” the direction is correct.

If the feeling is “this is a nice AI website,” the direction is still wrong.
